Research

Research Statement

My research lies in geometric group theory and its connections with low-dimensional topology, surface dynamics, and symplectic geometry. I am particularly interested in understanding groups arising from dynamics and symplectic geometry through their actions on hyperbolic spaces. Recently, I have been increasingly attracted to the interactions between geometric group theory and probability, with a special focus on random walks on groups via their actions on hyperbolic spaces.

Current Projects

  • Balanced curves, quasimorphisms, and the equator conjecture: joint work with Richard Webb on new geometric group theoretic tools for studying Hamiltonian diffeomorphisms of the two-sphere.
  • Combinatorial tools for Hamiltonian diffeomorphisms of the two-sphere: broader doctoral work on structures and methods connecting symplectic geometry with geometric group theory.
